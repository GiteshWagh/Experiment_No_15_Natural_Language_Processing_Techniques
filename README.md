## 3. Concepts Used

### 3.1 Natural Language Processing (NLP)
Natural Language Processing is a branch of artificial intelligence that enables computers to understand, interpret, and process human language.

### 3.2 Tokenization
Tokenization is the process of breaking text into smaller units such as words or sentences.

### 3.3 Stop Words
Stop words are commonly used words (like "is", "the", "and") that do not add significant meaning to the text and are often removed.

### 3.4 Stemming
Stemming reduces words to their root form by removing suffixes.

### 3.5 Lemmatization
Lemmatization converts words into their base or dictionary form.

### 3.6 Part-of-Speech (POS) Tagging
POS tagging assigns grammatical labels such as noun, verb, adjective, etc., to each word.

### 3.7 Frequency Distribution
Frequency distribution counts how often each word appears in the text.

## 4. Theory

### 4.1 Introduction to Natural Language Processing

Natural Language Processing (NLP) is widely used in applications such as chatbots, search engines, speech recognition, and text analysis. Since human language is unstructured, NLP techniques are used to convert it into a structured format that computers can understand.

---

### 4.2 Tokenization

Tokenization is the first step in text processing. It divides text into smaller components:

**Types of Tokenization:**
- **Word Tokenization:** Splits text into individual words.
- **Sentence Tokenization:** Splits text into sentences.

#### Importance:
- Helps in breaking down complex text.
- Forms the foundation for further NLP processing.

---

### 4.3 Stop Word Removal

Stop words are frequently occurring words that do not carry significant meaning in analysis.

#### Examples:
- is, the, in, on, at

#### Importance:
- Reduces data size.
- Improves processing efficiency.
- Focuses only on meaningful words.

---

### 4.4 Stemming

Stemming is the process of reducing words to their root form by removing suffixes.

#### Example:
- Playing → Play  
- Studies → Studi  

#### Characteristics:
- Fast and simple.
- May produce non-dictionary words.

---

### 4.5 Lemmatization

Lemmatization converts words into their proper base form using vocabulary and grammar rules.

#### Example:
- Running → Run  
- Better → Good  

#### Characteristics:
- Produces meaningful words.
- More accurate than stemming.

---

### 4.6 Part-of-Speech (POS) Tagging

POS tagging identifies the grammatical role of each word in a sentence.

#### Common Tags:
- Noun (NN)
- Proper Noun (NNP)
- Verb (VB)
- Adjective (JJ)
- Determiner (DT)

#### Importance:
- Helps in understanding sentence structure.
- Useful in grammar analysis and text understanding.

---

### 4.7 Frequency Distribution

Frequency distribution counts how often each word appears in a text.

#### Importance:
- Identifies important or commonly used words.
- Useful in keyword extraction.
- Helps in text summarization and analysis.

---

### 4.8 Importance of NLP Techniques

- Converts unstructured text into structured data  
- Enables machines to understand human language  
- Improves text analysis and information retrieval  
- Used in applications like chatbots, translators, and recommendation systems  
- Helps in sentiment analysis and document classification
